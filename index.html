What is Artificial Intelligence?

Artificial Intelligence (AI) is the simulation of human intelligence in machines that are programmed to think, learn, and make decisions. These systems can perform tasks that normally require human intelligence, such as recognizing speech, understanding language, making decisions, or identifying objects in images. AI is not just about robots or futuristic machines‚Äîit‚Äôs already embedded in your daily life. Virtual assistants like Siri or Alexa, personalized recommendations on YouTube or Netflix, and even spam filters in your email‚Äîall use AI.

AI can be categorized into two main types: Narrow AI, which is designed for a specific task (like a chatbot), and General AI, which would perform any intellectual task a human can do (this is still a theoretical concept). Real-world applications of AI include healthcare diagnosis systems, financial fraud detection, autonomous vehicles, and even agriculture for predicting crop yields.
